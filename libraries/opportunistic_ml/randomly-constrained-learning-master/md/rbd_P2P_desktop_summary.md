The following ranks the best performing choices of loss and resampling schemes
on the P2P_desktop table in the rbd24 dataset.
The proportion of positive samples in the training and test datasets is $\approx 0.21$.

The cost ratio describes a fixed trade-off between the cost of a false positive and the cost of a false negative, with the cost as a function of the false positive rate $\verb|fp|$ and false negative rate $\verb|fn|$ given by
$$
\verb|cost|=r^{-\tfrac12}\verb|fp|+r^{\tfrac12}\verb|fn|,
$$
For example, a cost ratio of 10 means that a false negative is ten times as costly as the cost of a false positive.

The loss parameter is a pair $(\alpha,\beta)$ where $\alpha$ and $\beta$ describe the asymptotics of the loss function
The fp and fn columns denote the sampled probabilities of false positive and a false negative outcomes respectively.

Notably,
- In all situations, methods using resampling schemes are represented amongst
the best performing approaches, but their performance is relatively minor
comparing to the best performing schemes without resampling
- in all but one of the top performing schemes with no resampling, we have
$\alpha\leq\beta$.  This isn't any precise confirmation of theory but in line
with the intuition that larger values of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ correspond to
lower risks associated with false negatives and false positives.
